## Bribery

 
 1. <i>Bribery</i> may be played after the speaker has abstained, or would vote if they are unable to.
 2. A player may spend zero trade goods when they play <i>Bribery</i>.
 3. A player must have cast votes for an outcome to play <i>Bribery</i>, and the additional votes must be cast for the same outcome.

## Construction Rider

 
 1. After playing a <i>Construction Rider</i>, a player may use additional abilities, including playing other rider cards.
 
    1. Any other riders may be played to predict the same or a different outcome.

 2. The effect of a successful <i>Construction Rider</i> will be resolved after the effect of the agenda.
 3. A player who cannot vote may still play a <i>Construction Rider</i>.
 4. If a player wishes to place a space dock, but there are none left in their reinforcements, they may remove a space dock from any system that does not contain one of their command tokens and place that instead.
 5. The Saar player may play a <i>Construction Rider</i>, and may place a space dock in a system containing a planet they control if successful.
 6. If a player successfully resolves a <i>Construction Rider</i> while they own the <i>Minister of Industry</i> law, they use the Production ability of their units in the system where they place their space dock. They may exhaust planets for resources, but those planets cannot be used to cast votes for the remainder of the agenda phase.

## Deadly Plot

 
 1. Any prediction made for a rider or similar ability is incorrect, and will not resolve.
 2. A player cannot make the prediction that <i>Deadly Plot</i> will be played when they predict the outcome of an agenda.
 3. <i>Deadly Plot</i> is played after any effects that allow a player to cast additional votes, and after any effects that change the outcome of an agenda.
 4. <i>Deadly Plot</i> is played before revealing the hidden agenda for the <i>Covert Legislation</i> agenda, and before the <i>Drive the Debate</i> objective may be scored. 5. If a player predicts multiple outcomes, they may play <i>Deadly Plot</i> regardless of which outcome would be resolved.
 
    1. If the Xxcha player uses their commander, Elder Qanoj, to vote for one outcome, when they have predicted another, they may play <i>Deadly Plot</i> regardless of which outcome would be resolved.

## Diplomatic Pressure

 
 1. The target player chooses which promissory note they give.
 2. Giving a promissory note because of <i>Diplomatic Pressure</i> is not resolving a transaction.
 3. A player cannot play a second <i>Diplomatic Pressure</i> targeting the same player during the same agenda.
 
     1. A player may play a second <i>Diplomatic Pressure</i> targeting a different player during the same agenda.
     2. Another player may play a second <i>Diplomatic Pressure</i> targeting the same player during the same agenda.
     3. A player may play a second <i>Diplomatic Pressure</i> targeting the same player during a later agenda.
 
 
 
## Direct Hit

 
 1. If multiple ships use their Sustain Damage ability, a <i>Direct Hit</i> may be played for each.
 2. A <i>Direct Hit</i> may be used whenever hits produced by a player’s units or abilities are cancelled by a ship using its Sustain Damage ability.
 3. When a player uses the Sustain Damage ability of one of their ships, they do not have to decide if another of their ships in the same combat will use its Sustain Damage ability until after their opponent has used or declined to use a <i>Direct Hit</i>.
 4. When <i>Direct Hit</i> is used outside of combat, if used on a ship with capacity, then fighters and/or ground forces must immediately be removed from the space area to meet the capacity limit.
 
    1. For example, say a player has a dreadnought, a destroyer and a fighter in a system with no other ships during the <b>Space Cannon Defense</b> step. During the <b>Roll Dice</b> step, two hits are produced against that player. They cancel one with the dreadnought’s Sustain Damage ability, but a <i>Direct Hit</i> is played to destroy it. The fighter in that system is immediately removed. The player must then assign the other produced hit to their destroyer.

 5. A mech cannot be destroyed by <i>Direct Hit</i>, unless a game effect causes the mech to be considered a ship.
 
 
## Disable

 
 1. If the Ul player is targeted, their Hel–Titans will still participate in the ground combat during the current invasion.
 2. If <i>Disable</i> results in no units on a planet having the Planetary Shield ability, then the ability of the <i>Magen Defense Grid</i> technology cannot be used during ground combat on that planet during that invasion.
 
    1. <i>Disable</i> has no effect on the <i>Magen Defense Grid Ω</i> or <i>Magen Defense Grid ΩΩ</i> technologies.

 3. <i>Disable</i> does not affect the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step, and is not played until the start of the <b>Invasion</b> step.
 
 
## Distinguished Councilor

 
 1. A player must have cast votes for an outcome to play <i>Distinguished Councilor</i>, and the additional votes must be cast for the same outcome.
 2. A player may only play <i>Distinguished Councilor</i> after their normal vote, not after they cast additional votes at another point.
 3. The speaker may play <i>Distinguished Councilor</i> after another player plays <i>Bribery</i>.
 4. <i>Distinguished Councilor</i> is played before any effects that change the outcome of an agenda.
 
 
## Divert Funding

 
 1. A player must have at least one non–unit upgrade, non–faction technology to play and resolve <i>Divert Funding</i>.
 2. A player must still meet the prerequisites for the technology they research.
 
    1. The returned technology does not count towards meeting the prerequisites.

 3. The researched technology may be a unit upgrade technology, and it may be a faction technology.
 4. The researched technology cannot be the returned technology.
 5. Other players must decide whether to play <i>Sabotage</i>, or otherwise cancel the action card, before the player playing <i>Divert Funding</i> announces which technology they are researching.
 6. If a player later gains the technology they returned, they gain it readied.
 7. If the player returns the <i>Antimass Deflectors</i> technology, their units may remain in and move out of any asteroid fields they are currently in, but cannot move additional units into or through.
 
 
## Economic Initiative

 
 1. A player with zero cultural planets may play <i>Economic Initiative</i>.
 
 
## Emergency Repairs

 
 1. A player may play <i>Emergency Repairs</i> at the start of the first round of combat to repair ships damaged in a previous combat, or during the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step of the current action.
 
 
## Exchange Program

 
 1. The player who played <i>Exchange Program</i> does not have to agree to place the infantry.
 
 
## Experimental Battlestation

 
 1. The selected space dock will use the Space Cannon ability during the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step of the current tactical action.
 2. Hits may only be assigned to the active player’s ships.
 3. <i>Experimental Battlestation</i> cannot be played if no ships are moved into the active system, even if the active player already has ships in that system.
 
     1. If ships are moved into the active system, ships that were already in that system may be assigned any hits produced.
     2. If a ship is moved out of the active system and then back in, <i>Experimental Battlestation</i> may be played.
     3. If all moving ships are lost to a gravity rift, and none arrive in the active system, <i>Experimental Battlestation</i> cannot be played.
 
 4. <i>Experimental Battlestation</i> cannot be played if ships are moved outside of the <b>Move Ships</b> step, such as during a retreat, or while resolving the Mahact player’s <i>Benediction</i> hero ability.
 5. The hits produced by the <i>Experimental Battlestation</i> space cannon rolls may be cancelled with <i>Maneuvering Jets</i>. 
 
## Exploration Probe

 
 1. A player must have an exploration token in or adjacent to a system containing one or more of their ships to play and resolve <i>Exploration Probe</i>.
 
 
## Extreme Duress

 
 1. If that player has the ability to perform multiple actions in a turn, their first action must be a strategic action to avoid the penalty.
 2. If a player has multiple strategy cards, at least one of them needs to be readied to target them with <i>Extreme Duress</i>
 
    1. If they have multiple readied, they may perform a strategic action with any to avoid the penalty.

 
 
## Fighter Conscription

 
 1. Only one fighter will be placed in a system containing both a space dock and a ship with capacity.
 2. A player may place a fighter in a system that is at its capacity limit. If they do so, they must then remove a fighter or ground force from the space area of that system.

## Ghost Ship

 
 1. A player may place the destroyer in the Creuss gate (tile 17), the wormhole nexus, or any system with a printed wormhole or wormhole token.
 
    1. Placing the destroyer in the inactive wormhole nexus will cause it to flip to its active side.

 2. The destroyer cannot be placed in the home system of an eliminated player.
 3. There must be an eligible system to place the destroyer in for a player to play and resolve <i>Ghost Ship</i>.
 4. If a player wishes to place a destroyer, but there are none left in their reinforcements, they may remove a destroyer from any system that does not contain one of their command tokens and place that instead.
 
 
## Ghost Squad

 
 1. <i>Ghost Squad</i> is played after the active player has assigned which planet each of their ground forces are being committed to. They cannot modify this after <i>Ghost Squad</i> has been played.
 2. <i>Ghost Squad</i> may be played whenever ground forces are committed, such as from the Yin player’s <i>Quantum Dissemination Ω</i> hero ability.
 
    1. <i>Ghost Squad</i> cannot be played in response to abilities that place ground forces on a planet without committing them, such as the <i>Colonial Redistribution</i> agenda.

 3. The Ul player cannot use <i>Ghost Squad</i> to move a Hel–Titan.
 4. A player may move zero ground forces with <i>Ghost Squad</i>.

    1. The Ul player will place each of their Hel Titan PDS undamaged.

## Lost Star Chart

 
 1. If the <i>Enforced Travel Ban</i> law is in play, <i>Lost Star Chart</i> will allow a player to move through wormholes during this action.
 
    1. A player may move from a system containing an alpha wormhole to a system containing an alpha wormhole, and similarly for beta wormholes.

 2. If the active system contains a beta wormhole, a player that has a unit with the Deep Space Cannon ability in an alpha wormhole may produce hits against ships in the active system during the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step of the tactical action, and vice versa.
 3. A player may retreat from a system containing an alpha wormhole into a system containing a beta wormhole, and vice versa, during this action.
 4. If a player has units or controls planets in a system with an alpha wormhole, and another player has units or controls planets in a system with a beta wormhole, then those two players are neighbors during this action.
 
    1. The Mentak player may use their Pillage ability against a player in this manner.

 5. A player moving a ship between two systems, both with alpha wormholes or both with beta wormholes, does not have to move through the wormhole, as the systems are adjacent. In particular, if one of those systems has the Ion Storm token, that player chooses if the Ion Storm token will flip.
 
 
## Lucky Shot

 
 1. A player cannot use <i>Lucky Shot</i> against their own units.
 2. Effects that cancel hits, such as Sustain Damage, cannot be used to prevent ships from being destroyed.
 
 
## Maneuvering Jets

 
 1. <i>Maneuvering Jets</i> cannot be used to cancel hits during combat.
 2. <i>Maneuvering Jets</i> may cancel hits produced during either the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> or the <b>Space Cannon Defense</b> step.
 3. A player cannot play a second <i>Maneuvering Jets</i> to cancel a second hit produced by the same Space Cannon roll.
 
     1. During the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step, each player makes their Space Cannon roll independent of each other player, in clockwise order. As such, a player may use one <i>Maneuvering Jets</i> after each other player makes a space cannon roll.
     2. During the <b>Space Cannon Defense</b> step, the units on each planet make their Space Cannon roll independent of the units on each other planet. The active player chooses the order. As such, a player may use one <i>Maneuvering Jets</i> for each planet they are committing ground forces to.
 
 4. <i>Maneuvering Jets</i> may cancel hits produced due to the <i>Experimental Battlestation</i> action card.
 
 
## Manipulate Investments

 
 1. If a player still has a strategy card because of <i>Political Stability</i>, trade goods may be placed on that strategy card. However, those trade goods will remain on that strategy card until it is chosen from the common area.
 
    1. If a player takes the strategy card with trade goods on it from the player who played <i>Political Stability</i>, using the ability of the <i>Imperial Arbiter</i> law or similar, they will not gain the trade goods, which will remain on the strategy card.

 
 
## Master Plan

 
 1. Effects that trigger at the start or end of a player’s turn will not trigger between each of those player’s actions.
 2. If a player’s turn is ended by a game effect, they cannot use <i>Master Plan</i> to perform an additional action.
 3. A player cannot use <i>Master Plan</i> to pass on the same turn they performed an action.
 4. If a player’s second action would be to use a component action to play an action card, and that card is cancelled, they are not required to perform an alternate second action on that turn.
 
 
## Mercenary Contract

 
 1. When a planet card is returned to the planet card deck, the player that previously controlled it will no longer do so. They remove their control token from the planet.
 2. If the planet card is returned to the planet card deck, then when a player later gains control of that planet, they will explore it.
 
    1. The planet will not be explored if it does not have a trait. If the planet is in The Fracture, however, the gaining player will draw a relic.

 3. If the Mecatol Rex planet card is returned to the planet card deck, the custodians token is not replaced. A player will be able to later commit ground forces without paying influence, and without gaining a victory point.4.  5. If a legendary planet card is returned to the planet card deck, the player that controlled it also loses the associated legendary planet ability card.
 
 
## Mining Initiative

 
 1. A player must control at least one planet to play and resolve <i>Mining Initiative</i>.
 2. <i>Mining Initiative</i> does not exhaust the chosen planet.
 
 
## Morale Boost

 
 1. The +1 will apply to any rerolls during the combat round.
 2. A second <i>Morale Boost</i> cannot be played during a combat round to give +2 to the combat rolls of a player’s units.
 3. <i>Morale Boost</i> has no effect on anti–fighter barrage rolls.
 
 
## Nav Suite

 
 1. Wormholes are not anomalies.
 2. An anomaly will retain its regular effect if a player retreats.
 3. A player may move ships through or into an asteroid field without the <i>Antimass Deflectors</i> technology. After moving into, those ships may remain there and move out during a later action without harm.
 4. A player may move ships through or out of a gravity rift without rolling for removal. Those ships will not get +1 to their move value.
 5. A player may move ships through a nebula. Ships moving out of a nebula retain their normal move value.
 6. A player may move ships through or into a supernova. After moving into, those ships may remain there and move out during a later action without harm.
 7. Other players cannot ignore the effect of anomalies during this tactical action.
 
    1. The Naalu player will still be affected by anomalies if they use their Foresight faction ability.

 
 
## Overrule

## Parley

 2. The returned ground forces cannot be committed to another planet during the same tactical action.

## Political Stability

 3. The “Return Strategy Cards” step is the last step of the status phase. All other steps must be completed by all players before <i>Political Stability</i> may be played. This includes scoring and revealing objectives, drawing action cards (and discarding down to hand limit, if necessary), and gaining and redistributing command tokens.
 
    1. If a player draws <i>Political Stability</i> during the status phase, they may play it during the same phase.

## Public Disgrace

## Puppets On A String

 
 1. The player who played <i>Puppets On A String</i> is considered the active player during their action.
 2. It is not considered the turn of the player who played <i>Puppets On A String</i> for game effects.
 
     1. They cannot use any “at the start/end of your turn” abilities.
     2. If the Yssaril player plays <i>Puppets On A String</i>, and owns their Transparasteel Plating faction technology, passed players may still play action cards during the action.
 
 3. The active player may use any “before/after you perform an action” abilities.
 4. Abilities that would end the active player’s turn instead end their action.
 5. The active player cannot use the Fleet Logistics technology to perform an additional action.
 
     1. The active player may use the <i>Master Plan</i> action card or the <i>Minister of War</i> agenda to perform an additional action.
     2. If the active player performs a component action, they may then use the ability of Suffi An, the Keleres commander, to perform additional actions.
 
 6. The active player is still considered passed during their action. They do not pass at the end of the action.
 
     1. The active player cannot score the <i>Prove Endurance</i> secret objective at the end of their action.

## Reactor Meltdown

 
 1. A player cannot destroy their own space dock with <i>Reactor Meltdown</i>.
 2. At least one space dock belonging to another player must exist on the game board in a non–home system for a player to play and resolve <i>Reactor Meltdown</i>.

## Refit Troops

 
 1. A player must have at least one infantry on the game board to play and resolve <i>Refit Troops</i>.
 2. If a player has no mechs left in their reinforcements, they may remove a mech from any system that does not contain one of their command tokens and place that instead. The mech will be placed undamaged.
 
 
## Reflective Shielding

 
 1. If <i>Reflective Shielding</i> is played after a ship uses its Sustain Damage ability during an <b>Assign Hits</b> step, then the hits produced by <i>Reflective Shielding</i> are added to any other hits produced in the immediately proceeding <b>Roll Dice</b> step.
 
    1. A unit that used its Sustain Damage ability to cancel some of those other hits, and been repaired, cannot use its Sustain Damage ability again to cancel the hits produced by <i>Reflective Shielding</i>.

 2. If <i>Reflective Shielding</i> is played after a ship uses its Sustain Damage ability at any other time during combat (for example, when the Creuss player uses their <i>Dimensional Splicer</i> faction technology ability), the hits are assigned immediately.
 
 
## Reparations

 
 1. <i>Reparations</i> may be played by a player with no exhausted planets, or when they lose control of a planet to a player with no readied planets.
 
    1. <i>Reparations</i> cannot be played by a player with no exhausted planets when they lose control of a planet to a player with no readied planets.

 
 
## Repeal Law

## Sabotage

 
 1. If a player has a second copy of an action card, they may play it if the first was cancelled by <i>Sabotage</i>.
 2. If an action card is cancelled by <i>Sabotage</i>, no costs are paid.
 3. A player playing an action card announces the following before <i>Sabotage</i> may be played:
 
     1. Any targeted planets, system, units, players, cards, outcomes, and/or other such objects.
     2. The value of any variable cost to be paid.
 
 4. Any dice are rolled and any cards are drawn only after all other players has declined to play <i>Sabotage</i>.
 5. If an action card instructs a player to research a technology, that player need not announce what technology they are researching until all other players has declined to play <i>Sabotage</i>.
 6. If an action card is played that will have an effect later, such as a rider, that card may only be cancelled by <i>Sabotage</i> when it is originally played.
 7. If the action card that was cancelled by <i>Sabotage</i> was to be used to perform a component action, the active player must perform a different action, or pass.
 
    1. If the component action was to be the second action the active player was to perform on their turn, through an effect such as the <i>Fleet Logistics</i> technology, the active player may end their turn without performing a second action and without passing.
