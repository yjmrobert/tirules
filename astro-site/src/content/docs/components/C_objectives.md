## Achieve Supremacy

 
 1. The flagship or war sun may be in the home system of an eliminated player.
 
 
## Adapt New Strategies

 
 1. A <i>Valefar Assimilator</i> technology will count if it is currently copying another player’s faction technology.
 
 
## Amass Wealth

 
 1. The influence, resources and trade goods must be spent during the status phase. Any that were spent during the action phase will have no effect.
 2. A planet may be exhausted to spend either its resource or influence value, but not both.
 3. Trade goods may be spent as influence or resources when scoring <i>Amass Wealth</i>.
 
 
## Become a Legend

 
 1. The number of units in the system is irrelevant, if there is at least one.
 2. A ship, ground force, or structure will all qualify for <i>Become a Legend</i>.
 
     1. If one player has a ground force or structure on a planet in a qualifying system, and another player has a ship in the same system, both players may count the system towards the requirements of <i>Become a Legend</i>.
     2. A player may have ships in the system containing a legendary planet or Mecatol Rex without controlling the planet for that system to count towards the requirements of <i>Become a Legend</i>.
 
 3. If Mirage is placed in an anomaly, or the <i>Nano–Forge</i> relic is attached to a planet in an anomaly, that system will only count as one system towards the requirements of <i>Become a Legend</i>.
 4. Any system containing a Vuil’raith player’s Dimensional Tear is a gravity rift, and thus, an anomaly.
 
    1. If a Vuil’raith player’s Dimensional Tear is in a system containing another anomaly, a legendary planet, or Mecatol Rex, that system will only count as one system towards the requirements of <i>Become a Legend</i>.

 
 
## Become a Martyr

 
 1. The lost planet may be in any player’s home system. It does not have to be a planet in the home system of the player scoring <i>Become a Martyr</i>.
 
    1. The lost planet may be in the home system of an eliminated player.

 2. If the lost planet is the last planet a player controls, that player will be eliminated before they score <i>Become a Martyr</i>, if they meet the other requirements for elimination.
 3. If the player with the <i>Shard of the Throne</i> relic loses their home system, they will lose the <i>Shard of the Throne</i> card and the associated victory point before they may score <i>Become a Martyr</i>.
 4. The attacker will have the opportunity to score a secret objective before the defender has the opportunity to score <i>Become a Martyr</i>.

 2. Any ground forces or structures are irrelevant.

## Demonstrate Your Power

 
 1. A player need not win the combat to score <i>Demonstrate Your Power</i>; they are able to score it if their opponent plays the <i>Skilled Retreat</i> action card.
 2. A player involved in a combat due to the Mahact player’s <i>Benediction</i> hero ability may score <i>Demonstrate Your Power</i> after that combat.
 3. Units that are temporarily ships for the duration of a space combat, such as those made so by the Nekro player’s flagship, <i>The Alastor</i>, or the Naaz–Rokha player’s Z–Grav Eidolon mechs, may count towards scoring <i>Demonstrate Your Power</i>.
 4. The Mentak player may use the <i>Sleeper Cell</i> ability of their hero, during the last round of combat to place ships before scoring <i>Demonstrate Your Power</i>. However, fleet pool limits are also checked before scoring.
 
    1. The Mentak player cannot use the ability of their <i>Salvage Operations</i> technology until after the window in which to score <i>Demonstrate Your Power</i>.

 
 
## Destroy Heretical Works

## Destroy Their Greatest Ship

 
 1. If a player destroys their own flagship or war sun during combat, such as via the effect of <i>The Crown of Thalnos</i> relic, their opponent cannot score <i>Destroy their Greatest Ship</i>.
 2. <i>Destroy their Greatest Ship</i> is scored as soon as the war sun or flagship is destroyed; it does not require waiting until the end of the combat.
 
    1. A player does not have to win a combat to score <i>Destroy their Greatest Ship</i>.

 3. A player may score <i>Destroy their Greatest Ship</i> outside of a combat.

## Dictate Policy

 
 1. <i>Dictate Policy</i> is scored after the third law resolves. As such, the <i>Deadly Plot</i> action card will prevent any player from scoring <i>Dictate Policy</i>.
 2. <i>Dictate Policy</i> is scored before any rider action cards are resolved.
 3. If there are three laws in play at the start of the agenda phase, a player may score <i>Dictate Policy</i> immediately.
 4. If the <i>Political Data Nexus</i> Xxcha hero ability puts a third law in play during the action phase, <i>Dictate Policy</i> cannot be scored until the agenda phase.

## Drive the Debate

 
 1. Any “For”/“Against” agenda cannot count for <i>Drive the Debate</i>, even if the outcome affects only a single player.
 2. <i>Drive the Debate</i> is scored immediately before the agenda resolves. The <i>Confounding Legal Text</i> and <i>Confusing Legal Text</i> action cards will allow only the newly elected player to score <i>Drive the Debate</i>, and the <i>Deadly Plot</i> action card will prevent any player from scoring <i>Drive the Debate</i>.

 4. If a player draws <i>Drive the Debate</i> while resolving the <i>Archived Secret</i> or <i>Search Warrant</i> agendas, they cannot score it immediately.

## Fight with Precision

 
 1. Scoring <i>Fight with Precision</i> will count as that player’s single secret objective scoring for that combat.
 2. <i>Fight with Precision</i> is scored immediately in the <b>Anti–Fighter Barrage</b> step of combat.
 
    1. A player does not have to win a combat to score <i>Fight with Precision</i>.

 3. If a player’s last fighter in a system is destroyed, but an ability allows that player to then place fighters into that system, <i>Fight with Precision</i> may still be scored.
 
 
## Fight with Precision Ω

 
 1. Scoring <i>Fight with Precision Ω</i> will count as that player’s single secret objective scoring for that combat.
 2. <i>Fight with Precision</i> is scored immediately in the <b>Anti–Fighter Barrage</b> step of combat.
 
    1. A player does not have to win a combat to score <i>Fight with Precision</i>.

 3. If a player’s last fighter in a system is destroyed, but an ability allows that player to then place fighters into that system, <i>Fight with Precision Ω</i> may still be scored.
 4. Any effect that destroys fighters during the <b>Anti–Fighter Barrage</b> step may be used to score <i>Fight with Precision Ω</i>, not just hits produced by an anti–fighter barrage roll.
 
    1. For example, say a player plays the <i>Courageous to the End</i> action card when one of their fighters are destroyed during the <b>Anti–Fighter Barrage</b> step. If the dice roll causes their opponent to destroy their ships, and they choose their last fighter, that would allow the player to score <i>Fight with Precision Ω</i>.

## Turn Their Fleets to Dust

 
 1. If the last ship is destroyed using a <i>Direct Hit</i> action card, <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust</i> cannot be scored.
 2. If a player still has fighters in a system after assigning all hits produced during the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step, but is forced to remove all of them to meet capacity limits, <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust</i> cannot be scored.
 3. If a player’s last ship in a system is destroyed, but an ability allows that player to then place ships into that system, <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust</i> may still be scored.
 4. Scoring <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust</i> will not prevent that player from scoring a secret objective in the <b>Space Combat</b> step during this tactical action.
 
 
## Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω

 
 1. If the last ship is destroyed using a <i>Direct Hit</i> action card, <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω</i> may be scored.
 2. If a player still has fighters in a system after assigning all hits produced during the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step, <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω</i> may be scored.
 3. If a player’s last ship in a system is destroyed, but an ability allows that player to then place ships into that system, <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω</i> may still be scored.
 4. Scoring <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω</i> will not prevent that player from scoring a secret objective in the <b>Space Combat</b> step during this tactical action.
 5. If a system contains only fighters, then <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω</i> cannot be scored by destroying them.
 6. Any effect that destroys non–fighter ships during the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step may be used to score <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω</i>, not just hits produced by a space cannon roll.
 
    1. For example, say the Yin player has their flagship, the <i>Van Hauge</i>, destroyed during the <b>Space Cannon Offense</b> step. They may score <i>Turn Their Fleets to Dust Ω</i> if the effect of their flagship destroys all non–fighter ships owned by another player in the active system.
